Natural diversity of Bharat

Overview

India is one of the world's 12 megadiverse countries. It has 45,000 plant species and twice as many animal species. India's animal diversity includes:
8,61,696 insects, 21,723 fish, 240 amphibians, 460 reptiles, 1,232 birds, 397 mammals. India is also known for its diverse ecosystem. 23.39% of its land is covered in trees and forests.
Four of the world's 36 biodiversity hotspots are located in India:

  • The Himalayas
  • Western Ghats
  • Indo-Burma area
  • Sundaland
India has 33% of its plants that are endemic, meaning they are found nowhere else in the world. 12.6% of mammals, 4.5% of birds, 45.8% of reptiles, and 55.8% of amphibians are also endemic.

Exploitation of nature

India's biodiversity has declined due to a number of factors, including:

Habitat loss and fragmentation

Deforestation, urbanization, and infrastructure development have destroyed natural habitats, making it difficult for wildlife to survive.

Poaching

Poaching of tigers, rhinos, and the sale of Star tortoises are major wildlife crimes in India. Tigers are an endangered species, poached for their skin and bones to cater to an illegal market.

Killing for precious commodities

Killing for ivory and trapping of birds and mammals as collector's items, curiosities, and for rearing as pets has threatened other species.

Over-exploitation of forests

The over-exploitation of forests has raised the problem of bird life. Other factors that have led to the decline in India's biodiversity include: Hunting, Environmental pollution, Poisoning, Forest fires.

Preserving nature

Steps Taken By Government for Biodiversity Protection

  1. The Central Government has enacted the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The Act, inter alia, provides for the creation of Protected Areas for the protection of wildlife and also provides for punishment for hunting of specified fauna specified in the schedules I to IV thereof.

  2. Wetland (Conservation and Management) Rules 2010 have been framed for the protection of wetlands, in the States.

  3. The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Plan for Conservation of Aquatic Eco-System also provides assistance to the States for the management of wetlands including Ramsar sites in the country.

  4. Wildlife Crime Control Bureau has been established for control of illegal trade in wildlife, including endangered species.
